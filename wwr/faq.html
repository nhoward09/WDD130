What do I need to wear for white water rafting?

    Many people often bring items such as these:

  • Swimsuit or Quick-Drying Clothing: You'll want to wear a swimsuit or moisture-wicking, quick-drying clothing. Avoid wearing cotton as it retains moisture and can make you feel cold and uncomfortable.
  • Water Shoes or Secure Footwear: Wear shoes that can get wet and have good traction. Water shoes or river sandals with straps are ideal. Make sure they fit securely and won't come off easily.
  • Sun Protection: Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun's UV rays. You can also wear a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses with a strap to shield your eyes.
  • Wetsuit: In colder weather or if the water temperature is low, the rafting company may provide wetsuits. If not, you can consider wearing a wetsuit for added warmth.
  • Splash Jacket or Wet Top: If you're rafting in cooler conditions or during colder seasons, you may want to wear a splash jacket or wet top to keep you dry and warm.
  • Personal Floatation Device (PFD): You'll be provided with a PFD by the rafting company, and it's essential to wear it at all times. Ensure that it fits properly and is securely fastened.
  • Helmet: A helmet is also provided by the rafting company for safety. Make sure it's properly fitted and secure on your head.
  • Additional Layers: Depending on the weather, you may want to bring additional layers like a thermal shirt or rash guard for added warmth and protection.
  • Change of Clothes: After the rafting trip, it's a good idea to have a change of dry clothes for post-rafting comfort.
  • Secure Straps and Eyeglass Retainers: If you wear eyeglasses or sunglasses, use straps or retainers to ensure they don't get lost in the water.

Is white water rafting environmentally friendly?

    Absolutely! At our company, we prioritize environmental sustainability and responsibility. White water rafting, when conducted with a commitment to eco-friendly practices, can be a low-impact and environmentally friendly outdoor activity.

    Here are some ways in which we ensure our white water rafting experiences align with environmental conservation:

  • Leave No Trace Principles: We adhere to Leave No Trace principles, emphasizing the importance of leaving natural areas as we found them. This includes packing out all waste and minimizing our impact on the environment.
  • River Conservation: We actively participate in river conservation efforts. This involves working with local environmental organizations, conducting clean-up initiatives, and supporting initiatives that protect and preserve the rivers we operate on.
  • Educational Initiatives: We believe in the power of education. We provide information and tips to our participants about responsible outdoor behavior, conservation practices, and the importance of protecting the natural habitats we explore.
  • Low-Impact Practices: Our rafting trips are designed to have minimal impact on the surrounding ecosystems. We follow designated routes, avoid disturbing wildlife, and ensure that our presence contributes positively to the natural environment.
  • Green Certifications: We actively seek and maintain certifications or partnerships with organizations that promote sustainable and eco-friendly tourism. These certifications reflect our commitment to responsible business practices.

    • Our goal is to provide thrilling and memorable experiences while fostering a deep appreciation for the environment. If you have specific questions about our environmental practices or would like more details, feel free to reach out. We're passionate about preserving the beauty of the outdoors for future generations to enjoy.
